## Concept 1 — The Private Lounge

The brand world is a 1920s members-only club. Whiskey, leather, art deco geometry. The logo should feel like a luxury automotive marque or hotel insignia, not a sports facility.

### 1A: Aperture Mark

```
Professional logo for "247 GOLF" designed as a luxury automotive-style brand mark on solid deep navy #0A1628 background.

The centerpiece icon is a perfect circle in champagne gold #C9A84C, with precisely arranged concave arcs carved from inside the circle — like the aperture blades of a camera lens opening. These internal cuts simultaneously suggest the dimple texture of a golf ball and a precision optical instrument. The geometry creates a subtle sense of forward motion. The circle is 80px in proportion with mathematical precision in every arc.

Below the circle, "247 GOLF" is set in ultra-thin extended serif typography (Didot weight) in champagne gold. The "247" is tracked at 0.5em — extremely airy and spacious. A 0.5px gold hairline separates the icon from the text with 20px of breathing room above and below. "SIMULATOR" appears below in 40% smaller size, same tracking, in white at 50% opacity.

The composition is vertically stacked and center-aligned, like a Rolls Royce hood ornament viewed from above or a Patek Philippe caseback engraving. Total aspect ratio roughly 1:1.4 (portrait). Every element is weightless and precise. Flat vector — no gradients, no embossing, no metallic effects. The luxury is in the mathematical restraint.
```

### 1B: Art Deco Monogram Seal

```
Professional logo for "247 GOLF" designed as an art deco monogram seal on solid deep navy #0A1628 background.

The central element is an octagonal frame (a regular octagon, not a circle) with thin 1px champagne gold #C9A84C borders — an outer border and an inner border with 4px of navy space between them, creating a double-line art deco frame. Inside the octagon, the numerals "2", "4", and "7" are interlocked into a single monogram using thin serif strokes. The numerals share lines where they overlap — the vertical stroke of the "4" serves as the spine of the "7", the curve of the "2" wraps around the "4". The interlocking is elegant, not cluttered.

Below the octagon, "GOLF SIMULATOR" is letterspaced at 0.6em in thin sans-serif capitals, champagne gold, at roughly 30% of the octagon's width — tiny and refined. Above the octagon, the word "MEMBERS" in the same style acts as a subtle crown text (optional — the composition works without it).

The impression is a gentleman's club wax seal, a hotel concierge badge, or currency engraving. Think The Ritz Paris, Soho House, or a vintage Cartier hallmark. Portrait orientation, roughly 1:1.3. Flat vector, single color (gold) on navy, no gradients, no dimensional effects.
```

## Concept 2 — Precision Play

The brand world is a Swiss design studio crossed with a NASA control room. Everything is grid-aligned, data-driven, and mathematically precise. The logo should look like a technical instrument or an engineering blueprint.

### 2A: Flagstick Reduction

```
Professional logo for "247 GOLF" designed as a Swiss modernist mark on clean white #FFFFFF background.

The icon reduces a golf flagstick to three pure geometric primitives in deep forest green #1B5E20: a vertical line (the pole, 2px wide, 48px tall), a right triangle (the flag, 24px base, 16px height) attached flush to the top of the pole on the right side, and a perfect circle (the hole, 8px diameter) centered at the base of the pole. The three shapes are solid fills — no strokes, no outlines. The proportions are based on a 64x64 pixel grid with every element snapping to 8px increments.

To the right of the icon, "247 GOLF" is set in Space Grotesk Bold in forest green, with "247" and "GOLF" on the same baseline separated by one space. The text is vertically centered against the icon with 16px of gap. Below the full lockup, a 1.5px solid forest green horizontal rule runs exactly the combined width of icon + gap + text.

The overall composition is horizontal, roughly 3.5:1 ratio. It looks like a technical diagram or a Swiss railway timetable logo. Generous white space on all sides. Flat vector, zero decoration, zero personality beyond the precision itself.
```

### 2B: Blueprint Numerals

```
Professional logo for "247 GOLF" designed as an engineering blueprint on clean white #FFFFFF background.

The logo IS a technical drawing of the brand name. "247" is set in a bold monospaced typeface (like JetBrains Mono Bold or IBM Plex Mono Bold) in deep forest green #1B5E20, at large display size. Around the numerals, visible construction lines are drawn in green at 15% opacity — a baseline, a cap-height line, x-height markers, and small dimension arrows showing the exact letter spacing. A red accent dot (#E53E3E, small, 6px) marks the intersection of two construction lines like a reference point on an architectural plan.

Below the numerals, "GOLF" is set in the same monospaced typeface at 60% size with 0.3em letter-spacing, in forest green at 70% opacity. A thin green line connects the bottom of the "247" construction grid to the "GOLF" text like a technical annotation leader line.

The composition reads as a page from a brand standards manual — the logo and its specification are the same artifact. Think Dieter Rams product sheets or Massimo Vignelli's design documents. Landscape orientation, 16:9 ratio. Only green, white, and the single red reference dot.
```

## Concept 3 — Game On

The brand world is a pro sports arena. Locker rooms, broadcast graphics, championship trophies. The logo should look like it belongs on a jersey, a stadium jumbotron, or an ESPN chyron — not a golf country club.

### 3A: Integrated Shield

```
Professional logo for "247 GOLF" designed as a sports team crest on solid deep navy #0A1628 background.

The logo is a single unified shield shape — wider at the top, tapering to a point at the bottom, tilted 5 degrees forward to convey momentum. The shield is divided into three angular sectors by two bold diagonal lines running from upper-left to lower-right. The top sector is electric green #39FF14 solid fill. The middle sector is navy (matching the background, creating a transparent stripe effect). The bottom sector has a 2px electric green outline with no fill.

The numerals "247" are integrated INTO the shield — each digit occupies one sector. "2" sits in the green sector in navy knockout text. "4" is in white in the middle navy sector. "7" is in green in the outlined bottom sector. The digits are in ultra-condensed bold sans-serif (Montserrat Black Condensed style), sized to fill their sectors. They are structural elements of the shield, not floating labels.

Below the shield, "GOLF" is set in ultra-wide tracked (0.6em) condensed caps in white, at roughly 80% the shield's width. No additional text. The composition is portrait, roughly 1:1.4. This looks like an MLS club crest, a Premier League badge, or an F1 team emblem. Flat vector, sharp angular edges everywhere, zero curves.
```

### 3B: Broadcast Kinetic Type

```
Professional logo for "247 GOLF" designed as a sports broadcast graphic on solid deep navy #0A1628 background.

There is no icon — the typography IS the visual identity. "247" is set in an ultra-condensed, ultra-bold sans-serif in pure white, slanted 10 degrees right (oblique), so tall and heavy the characters nearly form a solid rectangular mass. The three digits are kerned so tight they share pixel space. A single bold diagonal line in electric green #39FF14 (4px wide) cuts through the "247" from the bottom of the "2" to the top of the "7", like a scoring slash or a speed trail.

Below "247", the word "GOLF" is set in the same typeface but at thin weight in electric green #39FF14, tracked extremely wide at 0.8em. The extreme weight contrast between the massive white "247" and the whisper-thin green "GOLF" creates the visual tension. The green slash extends below the "GOLF" at the same angle, tapering to a point like a speed line trailing off the frame.

The composition is tall and narrow, roughly 2:3 portrait ratio. This looks like a UFC fight night title card, an F1 qualifying overlay, or an ESPN SportsCenter bumper graphic. Raw typographic power. No shields, no icons, no curves. Flat vector, only sharp angles.
```

## Concept 4 — The Green

The brand world is a farmers' market, a craft brewery taproom, a community garden. Everything is warm, hand-touched, organic. The logo should feel like it was hand-stamped onto a brown paper bag or carved into a wooden sign.

### 4A: Botanical Tee

```
Professional logo for "247 Golf" designed as an artisan brand mark on soft warm cream #FDF8F0 background.

The icon is a single continuous organic shape in sage green #6B8F71: a golf tee that grows into a leaf. The bottom is a tapered tee stem (straight but with slightly organic edges, not ruler-straight). At the top, instead of a flat tee head, the shape opens into an asymmetric leaf with a gentle S-curve — like a young fern frond or a tea leaf unfurling. A single visible vein line curves through the leaf. On the tee head, a small circle in warm terracotta #C07B5F represents the golf ball — the only non-green element, drawing the eye.

To the right of the icon, "247 Golf" is set in title case (not uppercase) in a warm serif typeface (Lora Medium style) in deep forest #2D4A32. Below it, "Simulator" in the same face at 55% size in sage green, tracked at 0.2em. The text block is vertically centered against the icon with 16px of breathing room. A small terracotta dot sits at the midpoint between icon and text as a visual connector.

The composition is horizontal, roughly 3:1 ratio. It feels like a label on an artisan tea tin, a badge on a canvas tote bag, or the sign above a neighborhood café door. Organic curves only — no straight angles except the tee stem. Flat vector with intentionally imperfect, slightly hand-drawn line quality.
```

### 4B: National Park Stamp

```
Professional logo for "247 Golf" designed as a circular passport-style stamp on soft warm cream #FDF8F0 background.

The logo is contained within a circle. The outer ring has "247 GOLF SIMULATOR" set along the top arc and "GOLF ON YOUR TIME" along the bottom arc, both in a warm rounded sans-serif (Nunito Bold style) in deep forest #2D4A32 at small size, tracked wide. Between the text arcs on the left and right sides, two small botanical sprigs (three simple leaves each, just two curved strokes per leaf) in sage green #6B8F71 act as dividers.

In the center of the circle, a simplified landscape scene: two gentle rolling hills rendered as curved lines in sage green, with a single golf flag (a thin pole with a small triangular pennant) planted on the right hill. Above the hills, a partial sun (a semicircle with three short rays) in warm terracotta #C07B5F peeks over the horizon. The central illustration is minimal — three strokes for hills, four strokes for flag, four strokes for sun.

The overall shape is perfectly circular, working as a badge, stamp, sticker, or wax seal. The line weight throughout is 1.5px — thin enough to feel delicate, thick enough to hold at small sizes. Think National Park Service passport stamps, craft brewery bottle caps, or Patagonia label marks. Monochrome with the single terracotta accent. Flat vector with a hint of hand-engraved texture.
```
